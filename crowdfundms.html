    • Crowdfund Mainstreet

    Making it easier for mission-driven entrepreneurs to connect with the right community investors


    The Client
    Crowdfund Mainstreet was founded with the goal of creating a platform to connect like-minded entrepreneurs and investors.
    My Role
    Product Designer (Analysis & Research to Lo-Fidelity Testing) on a team of 8 designers.
    The Challenge

    How can we make it easier for social entrepreneurs to crowdfund their projects with like-minded community investors?




    Our main focuses were the landing page, onboarding and campaign pages, which we deemed to be the most essential parts of the platform.
    Design Goals & Business Objectives
    • Develop a two-sided marketplace with value alignment
    • Simplify the process of creating a crowdfund campaign
    • Make it easier for entrepreneurs to raise money through crowdfunding
    • Provide educational resources for both issuers and investors
    • Create a landing page that attracts potential investors to join the platform
    • Create a campaign page that provides enough information and incentive for investors to invest

    Research, refine, rinse and repeat

    Since we were building the platform up from ground zero, thorough research was critical in helping us understand the competitive landscape, the users we were designing for, and the problems that we needed to solve. Our extensive research laid the foundation for the framework of our design and the product roadmap.

    Painting a picture of the equity crowdfunding landscape

    The first order of business was to perform a market and feature analysis of the top 6 competitors in the market in order to understand how Crowdfund Mainstreet would fit into this space, and more importantly, differentiate itself. Through our research we discovered that Crowdfund Mainstreet had the opportunity to promote local, mission-driven businesses using proper educational resources and by making small-scale investing easier and less intimidating.


    Part of our research deck outlining 6 major competitors in the equity funding space.

    Getting to know issuers and investors

    We conducted 16 qualitative user interviews with issuers and investors to figure out the biggest pain points in raising money and the biggest barriers to investing. With this two-sided marketplace, it was important to find value alignment between issuers and investors. We came up with four personas - two investors and two issuers. Through our research, we discovered that users care most about accessibility and community.

    Issuers cared about:
    • A strong community & network
    • Finding the right investors who care about social impact
    Investors cared about:
    • Transparency/trust in investing
    • Friendly and unintimidating process

    Making the platform accessible and community-oriented

    In order to address our users main concerns, we focused on implementing:
    • Unregistered browsing access to explore campaigns
    • A low-friction registration and onboarding process
    • Community building features such as a message board, FAQs, live updates
    • Educational resources like learning materials and help section

    Creating a low-friction onboarding and sign up flow

    As part of the onboarding team, our main goal was to create a low-friction sign up flow. We considered several versions of flows, trying to balance quick & easy completion with gathering enough upfront information during the sign up process to best match investors and issuers.
    Key Considerations
    • Steps to completion
    • Information cost (number of fields)
    • Effort investment (number of decisions and activities)
    • Users can sign up as investors and/or issuers
    • Gathering enough information from users (to help with matching)

    Final prototype and validation testing

    Our validation testing showed a high level of trust and likelihood to invest. Both issuers and investors cited location of mission, internal messaging function, comment and updates features to be key factors in building trust and transparency.
