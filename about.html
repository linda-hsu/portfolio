about me

I care about making things that matter. To me, that means designing for impact and social good. Designers shape many of the invisible forces around us, and it's up to us to use that influence to make thoughtful decisions that extend beyond the product and short-term metrics.

I’m currently consulting early stage start-ups on a range of user experience and user interface projects. Previously, I studied Business and Communication Design at USC and Graphic Design at RMIT University. While working at Bovitz, I gained a deep understanding of quantitative and qualitative market research methodologies and discovered my love for interpreting data and bringing those insights to life. I’m excited about using my background in research, business, and design to meaningfully improve experiences and tackle challenges.

Some things I enjoy –– yoga, cooking delicious veggie food, citing information from podcasts, pretending to be outdoorsy, derpy pets, ceramics, sustainability, making spotify playlists, having existential crises, caring for and simultaneously killing plants, thinking about dystopian futures, musical instruments, and making lists.
