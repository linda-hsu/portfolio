about me

I care about making things that matter. To me, that means designing for impact and social good. Designers shape many of the invisible forces around us, and it's up to us to use that influence to make thoughtful decisions that extend beyond the front-facing product and short-term metrics.

I’m currently consulting early stage start-ups on a range of user experience and user interface projects. Previously, I studied Business and Communication Design at USC and Graphic Design at RMIT University. While working at Bovitz, I leveraged quantitative and qualitative market research methodologies to solve complex problems and bring customer research insights to life. I love using my background in research, business, and design to meaningfully improve experiences and tackle interesting challenges.

Things I enjoy –– yoga, cooking delicious veggie food, citing information from podcasts, pretending to be outdoorsy, derpy pets, ceramics, sustainability, making spotify playlists, hot sauce, having existential crises, plants, thinking about dystopian futures, musical instruments, and making lists.
